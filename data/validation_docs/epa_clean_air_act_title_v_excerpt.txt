EPA Clean Air Act - Title V Operating Permit Requirements for Coke Oven Batteries
Excerpt Section: Emission Limitations and Monitoring (Section 63.302)

1. General Emission Standards:
   (i) The owner or operator of a coke oven battery shall not cause or allow the emission of raw coke oven gas or volatile organic compounds (VOC) from:
       (A) Coke oven doors to exceed 5.0% of doors on the battery;
       (B) Charging hole lids to exceed 1.0% of lids on the battery;
       (C) Offtake piping to exceed 4.0% of offtake piping on the battery.
   (ii) Emissions during charging cycles: Visible smoke emissions from the charging holes during coal charging must not exceed a cumulative duration of 15 seconds per charging cycle across any single oven.

2. Monitoring and Compliance Inspections:
   (i) Daily observations shall be conducted by a certified inspector to monitor visible emissions from coke oven doors, lids, and offtakes.
   (ii) Fugitive emission leaks detected during observations must be logged, and repair or replacement (e.g. graphite door seal maintenance or charging lid adjustment) must be executed within 24 hours of detection.
   (iii) If a door continues to leak for more than 3 consecutive carbonization cycles after repair attempts, the oven must be taken out of service at the end of the current cycle for structural rebuild.

3. Recordkeeping:
   (i) The owner or operator shall maintain records of all daily observations, door leak detections, lid adjustments, and corresponding maintenance work order numbers.
   (ii) Record files must be preserved for a minimum of 5 years and be readily accessible for regulatory compliance audits by environmental agencies.
